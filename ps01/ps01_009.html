Notes for Mark Steven Zagaeski
Mark is the second child of Ted and Vicki. He attended Star of the Sea School in Marblehead through fourth grade, then attended public schools. He was very outgoing and had many friends. He was graceful and athletic, and played soccer briefly in school, but was more interested in theatre. He was actively involved in high school theatre. He graduated from Marblehead High School in 1973.
Mark attended Bard College in Annandale-on-Hudson, NY. While a student at Bard, he participated in theatre and dance. During one summer break he worked at an aerodrome in Rhinebeck NY as an entertainer in the weekend performances of World War I vintage airplanes. He joined an ensemble performing an experimental musical production by composer Elizabeth Swados. This production became an award-winning off-Broadway show called Nightclub Cantata. After graduation from Bard, Mark moved to New York City and performed in the production for a year. The following year, the production moved to Boston, and Mark performed at the Charles Playhouse for several months. The show was videotaped at the studios of WGBH-TV and broadcast locally, also.
Mark chose to continue his education and enrolled at Boston University. He studied physics and biology, and worked with a team that studied marine mammal audiology. He had the opportunity to spend several summers in California studying marine mammals there. These trips involved driving coast-to-coast in a vintage VW Beetle, which Mark learned to service himself.
During this period, Mark would frequently leave the Boston area for extended periods, but would return and take on activities and projects at home. One example of this was when he returned from a long trip and immediately threw himself into a house painting project at his mother Vicki’s home in Marblehead. Vicki was particularly grateful when he would take on these maintenance projects.
Mark’s academic research work gave him the opportunity to travel to Europe. In the summer of 1982, Mark made a bike excursion through Belgium, the Netherlands, and northern Gemany, ending in Denmark. On another trip, Mark traveled to the island of Ischia, near Naples, Italy, and was there at the same time that Vicki was visiting her relatives there. So far, he is the only person of his generation who has visited Ischia.
At one point in his academic studies, Mark moved to California and worked with researchers at University of California at Santa Cruz. Among other activities, he worked managing the marine wildlife sanctuaries along the coast.
Mark had girlfriends in college, after college, and when he returned to graduate school. He met Debra Norman while they were students at Bard. They renewed their relationship later, and in 1983 they began living together. They were married in 1985.
As his family grew, Mark moved frequently. He has lived in Allston/Boston, Marblehead, Roslindale/Boston, and Lexington MA.
After completing his PhD, Mark worked for a research company run by a close friend, Bill Dolphin. He also taught, and in the mid-1990s became a high school science teacher, first at Buckingham, Brown & Nichols School and then at Lexington High School. In 2012 he left that job and began a private tutoring practice, teaching science and math to high school, college, and graduate students.
Mark continued to be active in dance and music. He performed for years with the choral group Halalisa. He has been a regular participant in the Dance Friday event for many years. As an adult, he enjoyed playing soccer and ultimate frisbee. He enjoys winter sports, particularly snowboarding. He is a self-taught mechanic of bicycles and cars, and a self-taught carpenter and general contractor.
Mark has an adventurous spirit and has always been eager to visit new places, meet new people, and try new things.
Notes for Mark Steven & Debra (Family)
Mark met Debra Norman at Bard in the 1970s. In the 1980s their relationship blossomed. Debra traveled to Denmark while Mark was working there. They began living together in 1983. They were married in 1985.
Their life together was very much in keeping with the spirit of the times. They were interested in living in a wholesome way, were eager to adopt healthy diets, and were avid members of local food cooperatives and groups that promoted natural lifestyles. As their children grew, they were advocates of progressive daycare and pre-schools, and believed in child-centered education and socialization. They had many friends and were active in school and church organizations with like-minded families.
Mark and Debra made every effort to create family experiences and activities. They regularly traveled with the children. Every summer the family would spend weeks at a cottage in Mashpee, near Cape Cod. The family would also travel to Long Island to spend time with Debra’s father Dan.
Debra had a good relationship with Vicki. VIcki appreciated that Debra would sit for long conversations and listen patiently while Vicki explained a bothersome issue or relationship. Vicki always felt she had a sympathetic listener in Debra.
Mark and Debra also had a close relationship with Shirley. Debra would regularly travel with the kids to visit Shirley in Rhode Island. Shirley would often come to Boston, too, and Mark and Debra worked hard to make her feel welcome and appreciated. This wasn’t always easy, given Shirley’s temperment.
The family lived for several years on Fairview St and then South St in Roslindale MA. This put them close to where Paul lived with his family on Centre St. Debra kindly agreed to dog-sit for Paul’s dog Marcus for a time, who often chewed furniture and once ate a bowl of cookie dough and had doggie diarrhea all over Debra’s apartment. Debra and Sabrina got along well.
The family moved to Lexington MA in the mid-1990s. The house on Paul Revere Rd was small for a family of seven. The kids found friends and the house was often full of middle and high schoolers. Mark spent many hours on house maintenance and added a large deck to the rear. They generously hosted holiday family gatherings that overflowed to the outside. The trampoline and rope swing were pretty popular with the kids.
After about ten years in Lexington, differences developed in the relationship. Mark and Debra separated and later divorced. They have maintained a very close family relationship and continue to help and support each other in their separate lives.
