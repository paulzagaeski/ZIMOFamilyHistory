Notes for Giosue Enrico Agostino Mattera
Giosue, known as Joe or “‘Sue” (pronounced “soo-WA”), was the second child of Harry and Gulietta. He was born in 1917. He grew up in Swampscott and attended public schools. He graduated from Swampscott High School in 1935.
Joe learned to play saxaphone and clarinet. He served in the military in World War II. In 1947, while visiting relatives on Ischia, he played at a Christmas dance party with Romano Mussolini, son of Italian dictator Benito Mussolini. He also met Mussolini’s daughter Anna there.
Joe married Marie Opgenorth. They lived on Aspen Rd in Swampscott. They had two children, Philip and David.
Joe worked for his father’s gardening and landscaping business. In the late 1940s, Joe took over the business from his father.
In 1981, Joe and Marie separated, but they remained closely involved in the landscaping business. Marie kept the books and handled all Joe’s business and personal finances.
In 1974, Joe sold the landscaping business, along with the Hemenway Rd house and land, to Charles Walde. Joe continued to work at various part-time and temporary jobs. His favorite job was at the Suffolk Downs racetrack where he helped out in the parking areas.
In the 1990s Joe traveled with his sister Vicki to Italy to visit relatives there.
Joe spent his last six months in an assisted living facility. He died in 2005.
Joe was very friendly and very well-liked. He spoke with a slight stutter, but he learned to compensate and loved to have long, rambling conversations. He had a quick wit and could come up with very funny comebacks. He knew a little bit about everyone in town and was friendly with cops, public works employees, professionals, retailers, barbers, and hundreds of classmates and “townies.” He had a sensitive, emotional side which he did his best to hide. He developed a close friendship with his nephew Peter Zagaeski, who worked for him briefly. He tried his best to distance himself from the arguments between his parents and his sisters Nicoletta and Vicki. He much preferred to get along with everyone.
Military notes for Giosue Enrico Agostino Mattera
Joe Mattera enlisted in the Army in 1942. After basic training, he was sent to North Africa. He was singled out for his ability to speak Italian. When the Allied armies invaded Sicily and then Italy, Joe was assigned to talk to locals, to gather intelligence about enemy fortifications and troops.
After Rome was captured, Joe was transferred to the OSS and sent to France. He took part in clandestine operations to infiltrate and destroy enemy positions. Joe earned a [medal] for his actions in France.
Notes for Giosue Enrico Agostino & Marie (Family)
Marie Opgenorth’s father owned the Quality Pad manufacturing plant in Gardner, Massachusetts. Her father sold the factory to an Army Captain, known as “Mac”, who was commander of a unit in which Joe Mattera served. When Joe returned from Europe and began working for his family landscaping business, he needed work to carry him through the winter months. He was hired by his former commander and met Marie, who also worked there, while working at the factory
