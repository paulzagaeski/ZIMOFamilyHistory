Notes for Debra Norman
Debra is the second child of Dan and Shirley. She grew up on Long Island. She attended Bard College.
After moving in with Mark, Debra threw herself into being a progressive, health-conscious mother and community member. She put into practice many progressive ideas about lifestyle and child-rearing, and was an active participant in church and community organizations. She had an open door for dozens of friends and was very welcoming to Mark’s family. She became very close with her mother-in-law Vicki.
Debra also took on significant responsibility for caring for her mother Shirley for many years.
Once her children were grown, Debra sought work that aligned with her values and her caring personality. She gravitated to the Unitarian church, and developed a role as a director of religious education in Lexington MA.
After separating, she moved to an old farm house in Lexington MA. Subsequently, she moved to another apartment in Lexington, then to Concord MA, and in 2016 moved to a condominium in Gloucester MA.
Debra has a warm, caring, attentive personality. She make it easy for people to talk to her. She is extremely creative and is always looking for new and interesting people and experiences.
Notes for Mark Steven & Debra (Family)
Mark met Debra Norman at Bard in the 1970s. In the 1980s their relationship blossomed. Debra traveled to Denmark while Mark was working there. They began living together in 1983. They were married in 1985.
Their life together was very much in keeping with the spirit of the times. They were interested in living in a wholesome way, were eager to adopt healthy diets, and were avid members of local food cooperatives and groups that promoted natural lifestyles. As their children grew, they were advocates of progressive daycare and pre-schools, and believed in child-centered education and socialization. They had many friends and were active in school and church organizations with like-minded families.
Mark and Debra made every effort to create family experiences and activities. They regularly traveled with the children. Every summer the family would spend weeks at a cottage in Mashpee, near Cape Cod. The family would also travel to Long Island to spend time with Debra’s father Dan.
Debra had a good relationship with Vicki. VIcki appreciated that Debra would sit for long conversations and listen patiently while Vicki explained a bothersome issue or relationship. Vicki always felt she had a sympathetic listener in Debra.
Mark and Debra also had a close relationship with Shirley. Debra would regularly travel with the kids to visit Shirley in Rhode Island. Shirley would often come to Boston, too, and Mark and Debra worked hard to make her feel welcome and appreciated. This wasn’t always easy, given Shirley’s temperment.
The family lived for several years on Fairview St and then South St in Roslindale MA. This put them close to where Paul lived with his family on Centre St. Debra kindly agreed to dog-sit for Paul’s dog Marcus for a time, who often chewed furniture and once ate a bowl of cookie dough and had doggie diarrhea all over Debra’s apartment. Debra and Sabrina got along well.
The family moved to Lexington MA in the mid-1990s. The house on Paul Revere Rd was small for a family of seven. The kids found friends and the house was often full of middle and high schoolers. Mark spent many hours on house maintenance and added a large deck to the rear. They generously hosted holiday family gatherings that overflowed to the outside. The trampoline and rope swing were pretty popular with the kids.
After about ten years in Lexington, differences developed in the relationship. Mark and Debra separated and later divorced. They have maintained a very close family relationship and continue to help and support each other in their separate lives.
