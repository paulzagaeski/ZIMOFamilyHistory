Notes for Peter Zagaeski
Peter is the third child of Ted and Vicki. Peter was born in Marblehead and attended Star of the Sea School until third grade, then attended public school. Peter was close in age to his older brothers, but often felt left out of their play, and spent a lot of time entertaining himself. When his younger sister was born, he was further isolated. He had one close friend, a neighbor named John Krupski. In high school Peter began to come into his own. He was the only one of the three boys to have a steady girlfriend in high school. He participated in high school theatre and found close friends in that group. He graduated from Marblehead High School in 1974.
Peter was unsure of his interest in higher education. He found academic work uninteresting. He enrolled in several schools, including Emerson College in Boston to study theatre production. He studied drafting and thought about a career in architecture and in theatre lighting design. He enrolled in the Essex County Agricultural College, and later worked as a veterinary technician. He also worked for a time for his uncle Joe’s landscaping business. He developed a great fondness for Joe.
Eventually he found work in manufacturing. He worked for Lightolier in Wilmington MA, and later worked for other Massachusetts based manufacturers.
As his professional skills and responsibilities grew, Peter took on more personal responsibilities as well. In the mid 1980s, Peter and his wife Katherine bought a historical home in Salem MA, and spent years and many dollars renovating it. In the late 1980s Peter decided to assist Vicki by selling his Salem property and moving his family into her Marblehead home. His plan was to expand the living space by adding a second floor and expanding the ground floor with additional kitchen, bath, and dining room square footage. He and his family moved in, the construction began, and while the work was done well, it took much longer than had been planned. As it happened, the strain of this disruptive period ended Peter’s marriage to Katherine.
Fortunately, Peter was able to recover from this difficult period. He met Mary Leonard, who became his wife in 1991. Her support and their strong relationship helped Peter. They bought a home in Sterling MA on Pike’s Hill Ln. Their son Ian was born in 2001. They were able to sell their home and build a new, larger home in Sterling.
In the mid-2000s, Peter’s Massachusetts company was acquired by Kooima Company of Rock Valley, IA. Peter agreed to commute to Iowa to set up and run the manufacturing facility as it was re-located. He worked on this basis for about a year, then decided to re-locate his family to Rock Valley in 2008. He took on more responsibilities and designed new manufacturing processes for leading edge products. He is now a senior manufacturing engineer for Kooima and has been credited with a number of innovations and patents.
The 2009 recession made it difficult for Peter to sell his home in Sterling. He and Mary took a loss on the sale, eventually. After several years living in rented houses in Rock Valley, they bought property and built a new home, which they moved into in 2013. Peter considers Rock Valley a temporary stop, and is planning to leave at an appropriate time to live in a community with a less homogenous culture.
Peter has a razor-sharp wit, and has inherited the Mattera talent for skewering a foolish person or idea without being offensive. His practical, hands-on, no-nonsense approach to people and situations makes him an ideal source of good advice and thoughtful, unbiased opinion. He has an enormous appetite for life, and is extremely generous to friends and strangers alike when sharing good food and good company. He is extremely ambitious both professionally and in his personal life. He’s not willing to wait for “a better time” to do things—he does them now!
Notes for Peter & Katherine (Family)
Peter met Katherine Triebs through mutual friends. Their first time together, they stayed up an entire night talking. It was no surprise to the rest of the family that Peter was the first of the siblings to marry. He had always been the one most eager to settle down in a relationship.
Kate is adopted, and was looking for a calm, stable relationship with someone who would be a strong caretaker.
Peter took on that role. He became her protector. He developed a strong, empathetic relationship with Kate’s adoptive parents, Carl and Priscilla who lived in Manchester-by-the-Sea MA. He also became a good friend to Kate’s brother Sam and sister Emily.
Peter and Kate were married in her family’s church in Manchester. Their reception was at the Triebs’ home in Manchester, also. They moved into an apartment. Justin was born in 1983.
Shortly after, Peter and Kate bought a dilapidated historical home in Salem MA. The couple threw a housewarming party for themselves and invited the rest of Peter’s family. The meal they served was unforgettable—they had gathered leaves from a tree on the property and used them to prepare a “stuffed leaf” dish that was completely inedible.
They put in a lot of “sweat equity” to improve the house, including using lifts to raise the building and reset the foundation. Peter was enormously ambitious, trying to do the renovation work on his own, with help from friends, from Ted, and occasional professional services. (It would have been helpful to know at this time about the local family members who were in the contracting and construction business!)
Kate worked in a local business, and Justin was cared for during work hours by both his grandmothers.
After several years in the Salem house, Peter persuaded Kate to agree to a plan to move to Marblehead, into Vicki’s Lafayette St house, expand it, and create a more spacious home for both them and for Vicki. This ambitious plan proceeded well, but it took longer and cost more than had been planned. At the same time, friction developed between Kate, who valued private space, and Vicki, who was eager to be “helpful” by constantly involving herself in every conversation and activity, and who felt slighted and disrespected when she was rebuffed and excluded. It was an untenable situation that could not be resolved while they were living under the same roof. Peter was put in an impossible position of having to choose between his wife and child and his mother.
Kate left and the marriage was ended. Kate never reconciled with Peter or Vicki, and maintained an arms-length relationship. She moved to Oregon and remarried there.
Notes for Peter & Mary (Family)
Peter and Mary met through an online dating service. They were pleased with the results.
Besides being enchanted by Mary’s generous and warm-hearted personality, Peter was also taken with her large, boisterous Irish family. They made him feel welcome in an enormous clan.
They were wed on the hottest day of the year, in July 1992. The church was poorly ventilated, and the reception was held in a building where the air conditioning failed. Nevertheless, it was a grand occasion.
Mary developed a close relationship with Vicki. She always had patience to listen to Vicki’s recounting of recent incidents and to offer an opinion about why the situation might not be as dire as Vicki thought. Mary had the ability to accept things Vicki might say or do without taking it personally.
Ever since they’ve been married, Peter and Mary have made it a priority to spend time with family, no matter how far-reaching the journey. They’ve visited Florida and Montana many times.
