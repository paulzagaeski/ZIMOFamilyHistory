Notes for Luigi Mattera
Luigi (Louie) was the seventh child of Giosue and Nicola. He was born in Calimera-Serrara in 1895.

Louie emmigrated to the US with his older brothers Michele and Enrico (Michael and Harry). Lydia Iacono's father Michele Iacono, who lived in East Boston, sponsored Louie coming to US.

Joe Mattera (Louie’s son) recalls a story from Vicki Mattera: Harry was insanely jealous. When Harry and Gulia married, they took Louie on their honeymoon trip. Harry became ill during the trip and wouldn’t leave the room where they were staying. Louie offered to take Gulia out to see the sights. Harry accused Louie of trying to seduce her while they were out together. (Another version of this story: the illness happened during the trip from Italy to the US in 1914.) Harry always held a grudge against his younger brother, and the two were never on good terms after that.

Louie owned a neighborhood grocery in Queens. The store was on Rockaway Blvd in Jamaica neighborhood of Queens.
Louie was godfather to Lydia Iacono in East Boston MA. In the 1940s when Lydia and her husband visited Louie in New York, he gave Lydia $50, a big amount, then handed out bottles of homemade wine. Her husband didn't understand, kept asking “Why is he doing this?” Lydia had to let him know being an Italian godfather was a big deal.

Alison Coons: “Everyone adored him.”

Joe Mattera 2016:
“My godfather is Nick Giardina. Nick worked in (Louie’s) store. Said that Louie was great at getting customers to buy things they didn’t think they wanted. In other words, he was a marketing genius.
During WWII, his customers who were Italian housewives would complain because they couldn’t get imported Progresso tomatoes. They said the sauce from American tomatoes wasn’t good. Louie asked a distributor, “Do you have Progresso labels?” He put a Progresso label on cans of American tomatoes. The Italian housewives would say “Thank you—my sauce is better now.” He knew how to make his customers happy.
Louie loved opera. A customer liked opera, too, but he was a jerk. Customer didn’t like Leontyne Price. “Puccini didn’t write that role for a coon.” Louie blew up at him. But, at the same time, he’d learned to say “nigger” as a common word for black person since others used it.
Big baseball fan. How did he become a fan? Louie told Joe: “I came to America, so I decided to learn about America’s game.”
