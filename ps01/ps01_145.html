Notes for Marie Opgenorth
Philip Mattera:
My mother was born on September 16, 1919 in Sheboygan Wisconsin. A business man in Gardner, MA, Mr. McKnight, who made excelsior pads heard of my grandfather and asked him to make a machine that would make the pads. My grandfather said he couldn’t leave his family in Wisconsin to go there so Mr. McKnight offered to pay for the entire family to move and then to give my grandfather a position maintaining and supervising the operation of the pad machine.

She graduated from Gardner High School in 1938, she graduated number one in her class with an overall grade average of 94%. She was offered admission to MIT with a 75% scholarship; however, my grandfather said “I didn’t send any of my other children to college so I won’t send you either”.

During World War II my mother worked for the national weather bureau first in Concord, NH then Columbiaville, NY.

This is a picture of my parents Joe and Marie taken on Feb. 14, 2005 at the Atrium assisted living home in Danvers. This is the last photo taken of my father before he passed away in March. It was taken by a photographer for the Salem News and it appeared in the paper that day.
Notes for Giosue Enrico Agostino & Marie (Family)
Marie Opgenorth’s father owned the Quality Pad manufacturing plant in Gardner, Massachusetts. Her father sold the factory to an Army Captain, known as “Mac”, who was commander of a unit in which Joe Mattera served. When Joe returned from Europe and began working for his family landscaping business, he needed work to carry him through the winter months. He was hired by his former commander and met Marie, who also worked there, while working at the factory
