Notes for Nicolangela Felicia Mattera
Nicoletta was the first child of Enrico and Gulietta. When she was born, the family was most likely still in East Boston or somewhere near. At age 5, Nicoletta lived at 8 Shelton Road in Swampscott.
We have little detailed information about Nicoletta. She attended public school in Swampscott MA and graduated from high school around 1933. She had training as a secretary. In 1940 at age 25 she lived on Elm Street in Manchester, New Hampshire, and worked as a secretary at a clothing manufacturing company. Later in 1940 she lived at 659 Humphrey St in Swampscott and worked as a stenographer (659 Humphrey St was owned by her uncle Michael, so it’s likely she was staying with the family temporarily). In 1945 she lived at 25 Roslyn St in Salem Massachusetts and worked as a secretary at Sylvania, where Victoria also worked. In 1960 she lived at 8 Broad St in Lynn Massachusetts. In 1960 she worked at Dunkin Donuts. In 1962 she left Massachusetts for Florida, and spent the rest of her life near Fort Lauderdale.
Victoria said Nicoletta had a romantic relationship with a sales manager at Sylvania who was married. This relationship lasted for many years, and when he broke it off, Victoria said, Nicoletta was so distraught she suffered “instant menopause.” Her physical appearance changed dramatically, going from a full figure to being extremely underweight. This breakup and the death of her brother Harry Jr had a lifelong effect on her. Once she moved to Florida she began to send long, angry letters to Victoria, recounting the difficult relationship with their father Harry. When Harry died, Nicoletta did not come to Massachusetts for the funeral.
For more than ten years, she continued to send long letters and make phone calls to Victoria, disputing the distribution of her father’s estate among Harry’s three surviving children. Victoria finally cut off communication with her sister because Nicoletta could not resolve her anger. Victoria learned of Nicoletta’s death nearly a year after it happened in 1988, when one of Nicoletta’s friends in Florida sent a letter to say that she had died the year before, and included her cremated remains in the package. Nicoletta is buried in the family plot in Swampscott cemetery.
