Notes for Giosue Mattera
Giosue Mattera was from Calimera-Serrara, a village at the southern end of Ischia. The village, along with Fontana and several other settlements, sits atop a 1000-foot volcanic plateau overlooking the town of Sant’Angelo, the southern port town on the Tyrrhenian Sea. Modern maps combine these several villages under the name Serrara-Fontana. The combined villages are called a commune.
We know little about Giosue Mattera. We believe he was born around 1860. He married Nichola Iacono (whose name could be Nicolangela). He had eight children. Three of them emmigrated to the US—Michele, Enrico, and Luigi. His other children, Stefano, Marie, Felicia, Rossina, and Agostino, remained in Italy.
