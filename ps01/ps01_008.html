Notes for Paul Zagaeski
Paul’s earliest memories are of the family’s second-floor apartment on Hooker St, Allston MA. Paul remembers the family’s black cat, an infant seat surrounded by a play table, and a toddler riding cart. Paul remembers at age two or three, sitting in the riding cart and accidently pushing himself backwards out the kitchen door and tumbling down the stairs inside the cart. He remembers Aunt Helen and cousin Judith rescuing him from the upside-down cart.
Paul remembers moving to Marblehead in 1956. He remembers visiting his Mattera grandparents in Swampscott MA and seeing the greenhouse full of flowers. He remembers the fruit trees, the large patch of blackberries and raspberries in the open space across the street, and the mulberry tree in front of the house.
Paul attended Star of the Sea Catholic School in Marblehead from kindergarten to grade 5. He remembers riding the school bus daily, and in later years riding his bike the 1.5 mile route to school. He remembers the parish pastor Monsignor McCarthy mis-pronouncing his name and pretending to find a whistling bird in the pocket of every student. Paul has reconnected with classmates from the school on Facebook. In grade 6 Paul attended the Glover School, and keeps in touch with several classmates he first met there.
Paul felt a special connection to Marblehead, and really appreciated how its colonial history was so readily accessible in the 20th century. As a child, his favorite experience was the YMCA summer camp on the islands just outside Marblehead Harbor. As a teen, he really enjoyed riding his bike all over town, learning to sail on boats owned by friends’ families, and discovering out-of-the-way streets and houses in the old part of town.
Paul enjoyed sports. He remembers being dressed in an old-fashioned football uniform with a kid-sized leather helment while watching Notre Dame and NY Giants games on TV in the 1950s. Paul started playing organized baseball and football at age 8. By age 12 he was quarterback on a town midget football team that barely lost a championship game when he failed to make a two-point conversion with a minute left in the game. Sports remained his most important activity through middle and high school. He suffered season-ending injuries in 8th and 9th grade football season, but bounced back in 10th grade to be Marblehead’s “sophmore sensation.” The highlight of his athletic experience was in 11th grade, a last-minute loss in the Thanksgiving game against rival Swampscott. Paul scored on a conversion and caught a season-longest pass that led to another score. His mediocre Marblehead team was a minute away from beating undefeated Swampscott, but lost on a field goal by Mike Lynch, who would go on to quarterback Harvard and later became WCVB sports director.
Paul softened his “jock” image by playing in a rock band (briefly) and being drafted to act in high school plays. Toward the end of high school Paul stopped getting haircuts and grew a bushy mop of hair.
Paul entered Hobart College in Sep 1971. He was a walk-on defensive back on the football team and quickly made friends with one of the team’s freshman stars Craig Williams from Syracuse NY. He later became friends with Philip Rawlins of Bloomfield CT, and maintained those friendships long after graduation. Paul found college academics challenging, became distracted by extracurriculars like pick-up floor hockey, and was asked to take a year leave of absence. When he returned to Hobart, he had better focus and improved his grades. He graduated in 1976.
Paul returned to Marblehead and volunteered on the re-election campaign of Representative Michael Harrington of Salem. After the election, he was unable to find political work in Boston or Washington, and started a series of short-term jobs. By 1978, in a poor economic climate with extremely high inflation and unemployment, Paul moved to Rochester NY and found work in a federal job training program at the Monroe County Manager’s office. After a year, Paul applied to Suffolk University Law School and stayed there for a year, then withdrew and returned to Rochester for a brief summer position. He returned to Boston, found an administrative job at a Boston law firm, then shifted to take an administrative job at a startup playhouse in Salem MA. There he met Norman Marcus who served on the theater’s advisory board, and who put Paul in touch with a graduate program at Boston University in broadcast administration. Paul completed the 18-month program in Dec 1983.
Paul began a series of jobs in technology business consulting and analysis. Over the next 25 years, he steadily increased his expertise in computing and communications technology, and worked with dozens of large and mid-sized companies who sold technology or used it to operate their businesses. One highlight was participating in an executive conference hosted by Apple Computer at a resort winery in California. After a half-dozen years in a senior role at Forrester Research, Paul was laid off in 2009 as the Great Recession began. Paul set up an independent consultancy and continues to do project work for large companies selling technology products and services.
Paul is a self-taught computer and internet expert. He’s owned just about every type of device Apple ever sold, and has helped many escape the dark world of Windows. His avocations are time management, personal finance, business networking, family history, and coaching youth sports. He has always been athletic and hopes to continue golfing, biking, and yoga. He’s looking for opportunities to volunteer in the community, in politics, and in youth development. He enjoys reading contemporary and historical fiction, fantasy, and biography. His taste in music is centered on r&b, funk, and jazz, but has listened to his share of show tunes and a cappela. Paul has learned to appreciate high-quality-low-price wines from Spain and Italy, and really wishes he knew how to cook better because he so enjoys eating well. He has traveled to Italy, Germany, and the Caribbean, and is eager to visit the Mediterrean region, Scotland, and eastern Europe.
Notes for Paul & Sabrina (Family)
Paul and Sabrina met in September 1979 at Boston University. Sabrina was a freshman living at the International House. Paul visited Nancy Sabin, a friend of the Rawlins, who also lived there. Nancy introduced Paul to Sabrina in the living room of the International House, where Sabrina was practicing the piano.
Paul and Sabrina did not meet again until 1982, when Paul attended a theater performance at Boston University in which Sabrina was a lead. They talked for just a few minutes, until Sabrina left the after-party with her boyfriend Byron.
In September 1983, Paul enrolled in a graduate program at Boston University, and found himself in the same class as Sabrina. He worked as a teaching assistant for the class, and used this role as a pretense to befriend Sabrina. They shared another class taught by Berge Ayvazian, who became a mutual friend. When Sabrina graduated in May 1983, she left for a summer in Paris, with the intention of returning to Boston in the fall to find work. A few months after she returned, she called Paul to ask, “Why didn’t you call me when I got back from Paris?” The fact she had not told him when she was returning didn’t occur to her, it seemed.
Paul’s first date with Sabrina was in Spring 1983, when she invited him to dinner at her apartment in Brookline on St Paul St. She attempted to cook a Hawaiian fish dish and baked apples for dessert. The baked apples were burned, but Paul ate them anyway. Their second date was in February 1984, when she invited him to dinner at her apartment in Cambridge. Paul brought candles, and Sabrina’s roommate Vicki Escobar declared this was a sign he was serious about her.
Seeing Sabrina became part of Paul’s daily routine. He would leave his apartment on North Harvard St in Allston, pick her up in Cambridge and drop her at WGBH on Western Ave in Brighton, and continue on to his office in Cambridge. Occasionally Sabrina would stay at Paul’s apartment, but this was not common because the apartment was cluttered with his dad’s belongings. As time went on, Paul would spend more time at Sabrina’s Cambridge apartment. In 1986, Paul and Sabrina decided to move in together. They rented an apartment in Jamaica Plain on Beaufort Road. They lived there for three years, until 1989. They were married in 1988.
The wedding ceremony was held at Star of the Sea Church in Marblehead. It was a lovely May day. Paul’s college roommate Craig Williams was best man, Sabrina’s cousin Mayra Rosado was maid of honor. The reception was at Glen Magna in Danvers. A string quartet entertained guests during the reception. Many guests remarked the venue, food, decoration, entertainment, and weather all cooperated to make a memorable event.
They visited the Dominican Republic for their honeymoon. For Sabrina, it was a homecoming where she was able to visit cousins in several towns. For Paul, it was a stunning introduction to life in a third-world country. Among the many experiences:
Cafe con leche
Renting a car that could barely reach 40mph
Driving cross-country from Santo Domingo to Puerto Plata on a two-lane road (before a cross-country highway existed)
Being stopped and shaken down for cash by Dominican police
Eating lots of fresh fruit, but Paul also eating breakfast sausage that caused a violent two-day case of food poisoning
Staying at a resort and scuba diving in Puerto Plata
Buying a “coco frio” at a roadside shack and watching the seller chop off the top of the coconut with a machete
