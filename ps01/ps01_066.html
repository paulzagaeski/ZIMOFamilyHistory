Notes for Albin Kenjeski
Ted recalls that Uncle Albin was a wonderful man. He remembers thinking often that he’d love to have Albin as his father.
Notes for Albin & Rose Phyllis (Family)
According to Ted Zagaeski (interviewed 1991), Albin Kenjeski bought a building in Newton Lowers Falls in 1920s or 1930s, on the corner of Washington and Grove St, close to the town line of Wellesley and near the Charles River. The building had been an inn at one point and was converted to a multi-family dwelling. Ted said the building was owned “by a Jew.” He said the sales contract included a mortgage and monthly payments, which Kenjeski paid regularly. After many years, Kenjeski’s family discovered the payments were for interest only, not principal, and that Kenjeski did not have equity in the property. They sued and were granted relief from the fraudulent terms.29

Albin and Rose’s daughters bought homes very close to the building on Grove St. Katherine lived at 17 Moulton St.
