Notes for Stanislao Antonio Iacono
Antonio was born in the US. His parents returned to Ischia with him. His mother was widowed when he was 12. He returned to the US in 1922 age 16. Made several trips to and from Italy.

Alison Coons: Someone named Vincenzo married Restituta. They had, I think, 3 children, Mario, Vincenzo Stanislas (Uncle Ni), Brigida's father and Begina.

Alison Coons: Antonio was undisciplined as a young man in Ischia. His mother Restituta spoke to Giuseppina in Boston. Guiseppina said “send him to me.” However, he continued to misbehave and skip school. Guiseppina took him to a construction crew and talked them into taking him on. He learned to work with concrete, and helped build the concrete pilings for the Mystic River Bridge.

Ni, whose middle name is Stanislas (????why??) Immigrated from Fontana age 16. He wouldn't go to school or work and he was sent to live with my grandmother. She put him to work and he went back and forth to Italy a lot. In 1947 he returned to find a bride and married Emilia Di Costanza from Bironopane. Brigida was born a year later. Ni died 11/19/73. What a bummer, he was a character. At my grandmother's memorial in Boston he sat across the aisle from me and kept calling  Eisenhower Eisenhower at me. I couldn't figure out if he was calling me that because my dad was in the airforce or because that he pronounced my name that way. Adorable.

Paul recalls visiting as a teenager. Nino asked to look closely at his bushy hair, “I’m looking for the birds.”
Notes for Stanislao Antonio & Emilia (Family)
